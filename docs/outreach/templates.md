# Ticker Tracker — Cold-Outreach and DM Templates

> DRAFT ONLY. Leif reviews, personalizes, and sends himself. Nothing here is sent.
>
> CAN-SPAM and anti-spam compliance:
> - Every cold email template includes who is writing, why they are being contacted,
>   and a clear opt-out mechanism.
> - Leif must have a lawful basis to contact each recipient before sending any cold
>   email. Acceptable bases include: public contact forms or submission inboxes
>   listed on the recipient's website, prior relationship, or explicit opt-in.
>   Cold emailing personal addresses scraped from databases is not acceptable.
> - All templates are marked "PERSONALIZE BEFORE SENDING." A template sent verbatim
>   without personalization reads as spam and should not be sent.
> - These templates are a starting point for Leif's voice. Do not use AI to
>   polish or rewrite them before sending — HN and maker communities flag AI language.

sponsored mention.

the newsletter's site. Use that form rather than guessing a personal email address.

---

I read [Newsletter Name] regularly — [PERSONALIZE: one specific edition or topic you
found genuinely useful, 1 sentence].

stock and crypto dashboard for retail investors who track both asset classes and are
tired of keeping multiple tabs open. It unifies a curated watchlist with email price
alerts, a Finviz-style market map alongside a crypto world view, analyst ratings, and
news sentiment — free to browse, no brokerage required.

I think it might be a fit for [Newsletter Name]'s audience because [PERSONALIZE: why
their specific readers would care — 1-2 sentences, based on what the newsletter covers].

Would you be open to a tool feature or a brief mention? Happy to share a free trial
period, answer questions, or send screenshots if that helps.

If this is not a fit for your newsletter, please just reply to let me know and I will
not follow up again. No hard feelings.

Thanks,
Leif Heaney
tickertracker.info
[Leif's email address]

---

> Personalization checklist before sending:
> - [Name]: actual editor's name, not "team" or "newsletter team"
> - PERSONALIZE newsletter detail: reference a real recent edition you read
> - PERSONALIZE fit rationale: specific to their audience, not generic
> - Remove these brackets entirely before sending
> - Confirm you are using their official contact/advertising form or inbox

## General compliance reminders

1. CAN-SPAM (US): Every commercial email must include the sender's physical mailing
   address (or PO box), a clear "from" identifier, an honest subject line, and a
   functional opt-out mechanism. For cold emails, include Leif's address in the footer
   and offer a reply-to opt out ("reply to this email and I will remove you from any
   future outreach").

2. GDPR / CASL: If recipients are in the EU or Canada, lawful basis requirements are
   stricter. Cold email to individuals requires explicit consent or a "legitimate
   interest" basis that is documented and proportionate. When in doubt, use public
   submission forms (which are opt-in by nature) rather than cold email.

3. Never buy email lists for outreach. Never use a tool that scrapes emails without
   consent.

4. Disclose maker/commercial interest in every community post and every cold outreach.
   Do not pretend to be a neutral third party.

5. FTC disclosure: If any YouTuber or podcaster receives free access, early access, or
   payment in exchange for coverage, they must disclose it per FTC guidelines. Leif
   should make clear in the outreach that he is the maker (Template 2 does this) and
   that he is not paying for positive coverage.
